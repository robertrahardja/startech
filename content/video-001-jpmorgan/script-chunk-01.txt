JPMorgan Chase just did something most businesses haven't woken up to yet.

They reclassified AI from R and D to core infrastructure. Same line item as the data centers. Same line item as the trading systems. Not an experiment anymore, a utility.

The 2026 number is 19.8 billion dollars. Two thousand staff working on AI full-time.

Now if you're running a small or medium business in Singapore, your first reaction is probably "that's a bank, that's not me." And I want to push back on that. Because the lesson isn't the dollar amount. The lesson is the category change.

Here's what changed. For five years, AI sat in the innovation budget. The CIO had a sandbox. The COO had a pilot project. The CFO had a line that said "experimental, review next year."

That line is gone now. AI is now sitting next to the electricity bill. It's sitting next to the lease. It's a fixed cost of running the business, because the alternative is your competitor has it and you don't.
