Two. Pick the boring task, not the impressive one.

Every founder I talk to wants to build the AI chatbot. The AI sales assistant. The AI something that goes on the homepage.

Don't.

JPMorgan's biggest AI win this year was internal. Agents that read documents and pre-fill forms. That's it. Boring. Massive ROI.

Your equivalent: invoice scanning. Receipt categorisation. Vendor email summarisation. Pick one. Ship it in 14 days. Measure the hours saved. That's the case for AI in your business, not a press release.

Three. Build for portability, not for a vendor.

JPMorgan can lock into OpenAI or Anthropic and switch in a weekend because they built abstractions. Most SMBs build directly against one API and then can't move when prices change or models get deprecated.

Use a thin wrapper. Whether it's LangChain, LiteLLM, or a 50-line Python file you wrote yourself, make sure swapping models is a one-line change. The AI vendor war is going to get ugly in 2027. Don't be locked in.
